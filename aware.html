Beware of Quishing Attacks!

Quishing (QR Code Phishing) is a cyber attack where hackers use QR codes to trick users into visiting malicious websites and stealing their data.

How Quishing Works:

  • Scammers place fake QR codes in public places or send them via email.
  • Users scan these codes, thinking they lead to a legitimate website.
  • The fake site asks for sensitive information, like login credentials or payment details.

How to Stay Safe:

Always verify the source before scanning a QR code.
Use a QR scanner that shows the URL before opening it.
Avoid scanning codes from unknown sources, emails, or flyers.
Check for signs of phishing, like misspellings or suspicious URLs.

Example of a Quishing Attack:

An email claims to be from your bank, asking you to scan a QR code to verify your account. The fake website steals your login details.

Stay Alert! Think Before You Scan!
